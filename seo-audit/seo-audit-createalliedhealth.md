## What We Found: Top Issues

### CRITICAL — Blocking Your Visibility Right Now

#### 1. AI Search Engines Cannot See Your Website
Every major AI search crawler (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity) is blocked by Squarespace's default robots.txt settings. This means when someone asks ChatGPT or Google's AI "who are NDIS social workers in Sydney?", your website literally cannot appear in the answer.

**Why this matters:** AI-powered search is rapidly becoming how people find healthcare services. Being invisible to AI search means missing an increasing share of potential referrals.

**The fix:** Adjust Squarespace's AI crawler settings to allow access. This is a settings change, not a rebuild.

---

#### 2. Broken Pages in Your Sitemap
Three pages in your sitemap return 404 errors (page not found):
- /ndis-access-requests
- /case-consultation
- /student-supervision

Plus 6 legacy pages from the old site structure are still live and competing with your current pages for Google's attention. This confuses Google about which pages are important.

**Why this matters:** Google sees your sitemap as a map of your most important content. When 25% of it leads to dead ends, Google loses trust in the site's reliability.

---

#### 3. Your Homepage Has Multiple Competing Headings
The homepage has 4 separate H1 tags (the main heading that tells Google what the page is about). Google expects one H1 per page. Multiple H1s dilute your keyword signal and make it unclear what the page's primary topic is.

This same issue repeats across service pages — a "Take your first step" CTA block is set as H1 on 6 different pages, competing with each page's actual service heading.

---

#### 4. "Accommodation" Is Misspelled in Your URL
The URL `/accomodation-support` is missing a "c" in "accommodation." This means when someone searches for "NDIS accommodation support Sydney" (the correct NDIS terminology), Google won't match your URL to that query. This is a direct ranking loss for a high-value service keyword.

---

### HIGH PRIORITY — Significantly Impacting Rankings

#### 5. No Google Business Profile Detected
There is no evidence of a claimed, verified Google Business Profile. This means:
- You don't appear in Google Maps results for "allied health Sydney" or "NDIS social worker near me"
- You have no Google reviews visible to potential clients
- You miss out on the Local Pack (the map section at the top of Google search results)

For a healthcare provider, the Local Pack is often the first thing people see when searching for services.

---

#### 6. Schema Markup Is Essentially Empty (Score: 9/100)
Your website has two auto-generated structured data blocks from Squarespace, but they're nearly empty — no business name, no phone number, no address, no services listed, no founder credentials. This is the data Google uses to understand what your business does, where it operates, and why it should be trusted.

For comparison, a well-optimised healthcare site would have structured data for: the organisation, the founder's credentials, each service offered, FAQs, and breadcrumb navigation.

---

#### 7. Title Tags Are Too Long (Truncated in Search Results)
11 of 12 pages have title tags over 90 characters. Google displays approximately 60 characters. Your titles are being cut off mid-sentence in search results, which reduces click-through rates. The automatic " - Create Allied Health Services" suffix is adding unnecessary length.

---

#### 8. Meta Descriptions Missing or Duplicated
- The homepage meta description is duplicated (same text appears twice)
- Blog posts have empty meta descriptions
- Service page descriptions exceed the 160-character display limit

These are the descriptions people see in Google search results. When they're missing or truncated, Google auto-generates a snippet that may not represent your services well.

---

#### 9. "Sydney" Is Barely Mentioned
Despite being a Sydney-based provider, "Sydney" appears only once on the homepage. Service pages have it in titles but the body content lacks geographic signals. Competitors consistently mention Sydney, specific suburbs, and hospital names (Westmead, RPA, Liverpool) throughout their content.

---

#### 10. Only 2 Blog Posts Since 2023
The blog has only 2 articles, both from late 2023. Competitors in the NDIS space are publishing monthly. For a YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) healthcare site, Google places enormous weight on fresh, expert content. Two years without new content signals to Google that the site may not be actively maintained.

---

#### 11. No Privacy Policy
Australian Privacy Principles require health service providers collecting personal information to have a compliant privacy policy. Its absence is both a legal risk and a trust signal failure for Google's quality raters, who specifically check for privacy policies on healthcare sites.

---

#### 12. Broken Internal Links
The "Learn More" button on the Housing & Accommodation section links to the Mental Health Services page instead of the correct housing page. On a healthcare website, sending a vulnerable person in housing crisis to the wrong page is a functional trust failure.

---

#### 13. Copyright Shows "2023 by Liddy Creative Studio"
The footer displays a 3-year-old copyright attributed to the web design studio, not to Create Allied Health. This signals to both users and Google that the site hasn't been maintained since launch.

---

### MEDIUM PRIORITY — Optimisation Opportunities

#### 14. No FAQ Sections or Schema
There are zero question-based headings on the entire site. Adding FAQ sections to service pages would:
- Target long-tail search queries ("What is a psychosocial assessment for NDIS?")
- Enable FAQ rich results in Google (healthcare sites are eligible)
- Make content citable by AI search engines

#### 15. No llms.txt File
This emerging standard allows you to tell AI search engines exactly how to describe your business. Without it, AI systems must guess — and they often get healthcare services wrong.

#### 16. NDIS Registration Number Not Displayed
Support coordinators and NDIS participants look for this to verify provider legitimacy. It should be in the footer and on the NDIS services page.

#### 17. OG Image Is the Logo (Square, Not Optimised)
When your website is shared on LinkedIn, Facebook, or WhatsApp, the preview shows a cropped square logo instead of a professional 1200x630 social sharing image. Healthcare referral networks share links frequently — every share with a poor preview is a missed first impression.

#### 18. Geographic Coordinates Set to New York
Squarespace's default geo coordinates haven't been updated — they still point to New York City. This could affect local search signals.
