## Summary: What Competitors Are Doing That Create Allied Health Is Not

**1. Content volume and freshness.** Top competitors publish 10-30+ blog posts per year covering NDIS plan reviews, case studies, participant stories, and regulatory updates. Create Allied Health has 2 blog posts, the last from November 2023. Google rewards fresh, authoritative content -- this is the single biggest gap.

**2. Local SEO fundamentals.** Competitors maintain optimized Google Business Profiles with reviews, photos, and regular posts. The word "Sydney" appears only once on Create Allied Health's entire homepage. There is no LocalBusiness schema markup, and no structured data of any kind detected.

**3. Keyword-targeted landing pages.** Competitors create specific pages for every keyword cluster: "NDIS social worker Sydney," "NDIS registered provider Sydney," "NDIS plan management Sydney." Create Allied Health has good service pages for psychosocial support and hospital discharge, but critical gaps for high-volume terms like "NDIS registered provider Sydney" and "NDIS social worker Sydney."

**4. Technical SEO.** Squarespace limits control over page speed, schema markup, and URL structure. The /ndis-access-requests page has no title tag or meta description at all. The /disability-services page uses a generic branding title with no keywords.

**5. Backlink profile.** Established competitors have backlinks from NDIS directories, government health sites, university partnerships, and news coverage. Create Allied Health likely has minimal backlinks as a newer provider.
